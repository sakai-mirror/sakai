Gradebook: Overview

What it is

The Gradebook is a tool for instructors to calculate, store, and distribute grade information to students online.

Key concepts

Features: Using the Gradebook, instructors may:

  • Autocalculate course grades

  • Create and edit a mapping of the course letter grade to a 100 percent scale

  • Add, view, edit, and release point values of assignments and/or assessments to students

  • Manually enter, view, edit, and release to students their scores and grades

  • Export scores for an assessment from Tests & Quizzes to the gradebook, which creates an assignment in the gradebook and transfers student scores to that assignment

  • Export scores and grades to Microsoft Excel (in .xls or .csv format)

Viewing scores: The Gradebook will allow students to view their own scores and grades, once instructors have released them.

Things to consider

  • There are different ways instructors can view information in the Gradebook, including a general overview for each course, assignment detail, course grade detail, student roster, adding or editing assignments, and gradebook options.

  • Students can view their cumulative scores for all assignments and the corresponding percentage; view their course grades, once an instructor releases them; and sort their assignments by title, due date, grade, and total possible points per assignment.
