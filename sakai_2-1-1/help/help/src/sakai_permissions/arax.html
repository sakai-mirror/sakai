Sakai 2.0 Permissions and Roles: Overview

What they do

Permissions allow users to access certain features of a course or project worksite, depending on their roles and on the decisions made by the site maintainer and the system administrator.

When you create a worksite or when one is created for you, you as the maintainer of that site can choose (within the limits established by the system administrator) which tools or functions (e.g., Discussion, Schedule, Resources) you want the site to have. For each of these tools or functions, you can set permissions that allow or prevent users from seeing or performing certain tasks depending on their roles.

Key concepts

Roles may vary in name depending on the application implementation. However, in general, the "maintain" role (or its equivalent) has full administrative permission to create, edit, and delete within a worksite. The "access" role has fewer permissions, and cannot create content or delete in all tools. For example, by default the access role cannot upload files into Resources, but can create Chat messages and Discussion replies.

Roles are given default permissions. However, someone with maintain rights can change the permissions for different roles to suit the site's needs.

Things to remember

  • The availability of a tool in a worksite depends upon the particular implementation of the system administrator. If you have maintain rights to a worksite and find that a tool is not available through Worksite Setup, consult your administrator.

  • Default roles have default permissions; however, these defaults can be edited on the system level by the system administrator and on the worksite level by anyone with maintain rights to the site.

  • All users are assigned the maintain role in their respective My Workspaces; this gives a user edit control over his or her My Workspace.
